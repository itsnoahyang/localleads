Getting Started Guide

How to Get Your First Leads
in 6 Simple Steps

No experience needed. Follow this guide once and you'll have a list of local business leads downloaded to your computer in under 10 minutes.

1
Get your free Google API Key
One-time setup · Takes about 5 minutes

This app searches Google Maps to find businesses for you. To do that, it needs a "Google API Key" — think of it like a special password that lets this app talk to Google on your behalf. You only need to set this up once, ever.

Here's exactly how to get your key:

  • 1
    Go to console.cloud.google.com and sign in with any Google account (Gmail works fine)
  • 2
    Click "Create Project" — name it anything you want, like "Lead Machine"
  • 3
    In the search bar at the top, type "Places API (New)" and click on it in the results
  • 4
    Click the blue "Enable" button
  • 5
    On the left menu, click "Credentials", then click "+ Create Credentials", then "API Key"
  • 6
    A box will appear with your key (it looks like a long string of random letters). Copy it and save it somewhere safe — like a notes app.
📸 Visual walkthrough
Step 2 — Click the project dropdown in the top nav bar
Step 2 (cont.) — A popup appears. Click "NEW PROJECT" in the top right corner
Step 3 — Click the search bar, type "Places API (New)", then click the first result that appears

Step 6 — A box pops up with your key. Click "COPY" then paste it somewhere safe (Notes app, etc.)
Note: Google may ask you to set up billing during this process. Go ahead and add a card — you control your own spending limits inside Google Cloud.
2
Add your key to this app
30 seconds · Only needs to be done once

Once you have your Google API Key, you need to tell this app what it is so it can use it to search for you.

  • 1
    Click the "API Key" button in the top right corner of this page
  • 2
    Paste your key into the box that appears
  • 3
    Click Save — you're done! You won't need to do this again.
Your key stays private. It's only saved on your device. We never see it or store it on our servers.
3
Choose what types of businesses to find
Pick one or many service types

On the left side of the Scraper, you'll see a list of service types — things like Roofing, Plumbing, HVAC, Landscaping, and more. These are the kinds of businesses you want to find.

Just click on the ones you want. They'll highlight in purple when selected. Click again to remove them.

The more types you pick, the more leads you'll get — but it also costs a little more. The cost estimate on the right side of the Scraper updates automatically as you pick.
4
Choose where to search
Pick states, provinces, or a specific city

Under the service types, you'll see a map of states and provinces. Check the boxes next to the areas you want to search. Each state or province is broken down into its major cities automatically — you don't need to list them yourself.

You can also switch between countries (US, Canada, Australia, Europe) using the buttons at the top of the location section.

Want just one specific city? Scroll to the "Specific Location" section at the bottom left and type your city in. For example: "Austin, TX" or "Richmond, BC". Add as many specific cities as you want.
First time tip: Start with just 1–2 states. You can always run a bigger search later. This lets you test the results before spending more credits.
5
Check the cost estimate, then launch
Always free to estimate · Only costs when you run

Before you hit Launch, look at the "Cost Estimate" box on the right side. It tells you exactly how much this search will cost using your Google API key — before you run anything.

Most small searches cost less than a dollar. You can set spending limits inside your Google Cloud account to stay in full control of your costs.

When you're happy with your selection, hit the big purple "Run Scrape" button. That's it — the search starts automatically.

Nothing runs until you click that button. You can adjust your selections and watch the estimate update as many times as you want. You're never charged just for looking.
✓
Watch it work, then download your leads
Sit back and let the app do the work

After you hit Launch, you'll see a live progress screen. It shows you:

  • 📊
    How many leads have been found so far — this number goes up as the search runs
  • ⏱️
    How much time is left — so you know when to come back
  • 💰
    How much it's cost so far — live tracking against your Google credit
  • 📍
    Which city it's currently searching — a live feed of businesses being found

When it's done, click "View Results →". You'll see a table with every business found — their name, phone number, website, star rating, and number of Google reviews.

You can filter the list by name, city, niche, or star rating to focus on the best leads. When you're ready, hit "Download CSV" and the list saves to your computer as a spreadsheet you can open in Excel or Google Sheets.

That's it. You now have a list of real local businesses with contact info, ready to call, email, or hand off to a salesperson.

Find Owner Names

When you click Find Owner Names, the app visits each business's website and tries to identify who owns or runs the company — completely free, no paid APIs involved.

It scans the contact page, about page, and team page, looking for patterns like "Founded by John Smith" or "Owner: Sarah Lee" to pull out a real name.

Heads up on accuracy: Owner names are found on about 25–35% of leads. Plenty of businesses don't list this publicly, so many will come back blank — that's expected.

Any owner names found will appear as a new column in the table and will be included when you download your CSV.
